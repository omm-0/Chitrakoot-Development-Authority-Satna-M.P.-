About Chitrakoot

The Sacred Land of Lord Rama's Exile

Chitrakoot Dham, nestled in the serene hills of the northern Vindhya Range along the banks of the sacred Mandakini River, is one of India's most revered pilgrimage centres. Located in Satna district of Madhya Pradesh, Chitrakoot — meaning 'The Hill of Many Wonders' — is deeply connected to the epic Ramayana.

Lord Rama, Mata Sita, and Lakshman spent eleven and a half years of their fourteen-year exile in these divine forests. The Hindu trinity — Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh — are said to have taken incarnation here.

About Chitrakoot Dham

The Sacred Land Where Ramayana Comes Alive

History & Mythology

Chitrakoot falls in the northern Vindhya Range, spread across Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh. The MP side comes under Satna district. The name 'Chitrakoot' comes from Sanskrit — 'Chitra' (Ashoka tree) + 'Koot' (peak), as the region was once abundant with Ashoka trees.

It is believed that Lord Rama, Sita and Lakshman spent eleven and a half years of their fourteen-year exile in these divine forests. This is where the historic Bharat Milap happened — Bharata came from Ayodhya to persuade Rama to return to rule the kingdom. When Lord Rama gently refused, Bharata took his wooden sandals (Khadau) to place on the throne and rule in Rama's name.

Lord Rama performed the last rites (Shraddh) of his father Dasharatha here. Goswami Tulsidas composed major portions of Ramcharitmanas sitting at Ramghat. Ancient sages — Maharishi Atri, Sati Anusuya, Agastya — performed intense tapasya here. The Mandakini River itself was brought down by Sati Anusuya's severe penance.

Spiritual Heritage

Divine Significance

✦ Only place where all three Hindu deities — Brahma, Vishnu, Mahesh — took incarnation
✦ Bhagwan Kamtanath (Lord Rama) is the Ishta Devta of entire Chitrakoot
✦ Kamadgiri Parikrama Marg developed by Bundela Queen in 1725
✦ Evening Mandakini Aarti at Ramghat rivals the famous Ganga Aarti of Varanasi
✦ Chitrakoot Parvat Mala includes Kamadgiri, Hanuman Dhara, and Lakshman Pahari
✦ Ganesh Bagh is known as 'Mini Khajuraho' for its Peshwa-era carvings

Sacred Places of Chitrakoot

Discover the Divine Sites of the Hill of Many Wonders

Kamadgiri Parikrama

The Hill That Fulfills All Desires

Kamadgiri, the original Chitrakoot, is a forested hill of prime religious significance. It is believed to be the holy embodiment of Lord Rama. Devotees perform a 5 km parikrama (circumambulation) around it to seek blessings. The path features numerous temples, including the main Kamtanath temple.

DistanceCentral Chitrakoot
Time2-3 Hours
EntryFree
FacilitiesWheelchair available

Ramghat & Mandakini Aarti

The Divine Evening Aarti

The bustling Ramghat is where Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshman are believed to have bathed. The evening aarti here is a breathtaking spectacle, with priests offering prayers to the Mandakini river amidst chanting. It's the spiritual heart of the town.

AartiDaily at 6:00 PM
BoatingAvailable (₹150-200)
View on Map

Hanuman Dhara

The Sacred Spring

Located on a steep hill, Hanuman Dhara features a natural spring falling over an idol of Lord Hanuman. It is said Lord Rama created this spring to soothe Hanuman's anger and heat after he burned Lanka. The climb of 355 stairs offers panoramic views.

Distance4 km from Ramghat
Climb355 Steps
View on Map

Gupt Godavari Caves

The Mystical Caves

A pair of natural caves where a subterranean stream flows. Legend says the Godavari river secretly emerged here to wash the feet of Lord Rama. The larger cave features two natural throne-like rocks where Rama and Lakshman held court.

Distance18 km from town
FeatureKnee-deep water inside
View on Map

Sati Anusuya Ashram

Where Sita Received Teachings

Located deep in the forest, this is where Sage Atri and his wife Sati Anusuya meditated. Anusuya imparted the teachings of 'Pativrata Dharma' to Mata Sita here. The Mandakini river is believed to have originated here due to Anusuya's penance.

Distance18 km from Ramghat
AttractionBeautiful forest drive
View on Map

Sphatik Shila

The Crystal Rock Bearing Divine Footprints

Two massive polished rocks on Mandakini's bank bearing footprints of Lord Rama and Sita. This picturesque spot is where Jayant (Indra's son), disguised as a crow, pecked Sita's feet, angering Lord Rama.

LocationNear Janki Kund
SignificanceFootprints on rock
View on Map
